Jay Hanuman Dada

Hanuman (/ˈhʌnʊˌmɑːn/; Sanskrit: हनुमान्, IAST: Hanumān),[4] also known as Maruti, Bajrangabali, and Anjaneya,[5] is a deity in Hinduism, revered as a divine vanara and a devoted companion of the deity Rama. Central to the Ramayana, Hanuman is celebrated for his unwavering devotion to Rama and is considered a chiranjivi. He is traditionally believed to be the spiritual offspring of the wind deity Vayu, who is said to have played a significant role in his birth.[6][7] His tales are recounted not only in the Ramayana but also in the Mahabharata and various Puranas.

Ramayan

रामायण (संस्कृत : रामायणम् = राम + आयणम् ; शाब्दिक अर्थ : 'राम की जीवन-यात्रा'), वाल्मीकि द्वारा रचित संस्कृत महाकाव्य है जिसमें श्रीराम की गाथा है। इसे आदिकाव्य[1] तथा इसके रचयिता महर्षि वाल्मीकि को 'आदिकवि'[2] भी कहा जाता है। संस्कृत साहित्य परम्परा में रामायण और महाभारत को इतिहास कहा गया है और दोनों सनातन संस्कृति के सबसे प्रसिद्ध और लोकप्रिय ग्रन्थ हैं। रामायण के सात अध्याय हैं जो काण्ड के नाम से जाने जाते हैं। इसमें कुल लगभग २४,००० श्लोक[ख] हैं। सातवां उत्तरकांड प्रक्षिप्त है, तत्वमार्तंड में भी उत्तरकांड को प्रक्षिप्त माना गया है ।[3] उसके बाद की संस्कृत एवं अन्य भारतीय भाषाओं के साहित्य पर इस महाकाव्य का बहुत अधिक प्रभाव है तथा रामकथा को लेकर अनेकों 'रामायण' रचे गये।

Shree Ram

Rama was born to Kaushalya and Dasharatha in Ayodhya, the capital of the Kingdom of Kosala. His siblings included Lakshmana, Bharata, and Shatrughna. He married Sita. Though born in a royal family, Rama's life is described in the Hindu texts as one challenged by unexpected changes, such as an exile into impoverished and difficult circumstances, and challenges of ethical questions and moral dilemmas.[6] Of all his travails, the most notable is the kidnapping of Sita by demon-king Ravana, followed by the determined and epic efforts of Rama and Lakshmana to gain her freedom and destroy the evil Ravana against great odds.
